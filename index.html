IMPACT

For nearly 25 years, PLTW has offered transformative classroom and learning experiences for PreK-12 students that energize teachers and students alike. We help students own their education through active, hands-on, real-world learning, and provide career exposure and exploration to help them thrive in life beyond the classroom. With more than 15,000 programs in more than 12,200 schools across the U.S., PLTW is providing opportunities for all students to have brighter futures.

ACCESS

PLTW believes all students deserve access to a quality education that prepares them for college and career success. Whether students live in an urban city, a rural town, or anywhere in between, our goal is to provide them with the opportunity for relevant, engaging, hands-on, real-world learning experiences through traditional in-classroom and distance learning instruction. Our customizable options allow schools to implement PLTW programs based on the unique needs of their student population, and we provide funding opportunities through our school grant program. We help to build equity, confidence, and achievement, and believe race, gender, background, and financial status shouldn’t be a barrier between students and success.

 

CAREER READINESS

PLTW amplifies the potential in all students by developing valuable, life-long problem solving and communication skills needed to succeed beyond the classroom. Whatever future they may choose, PLTW students discover careers in biomedical science, engineering, and computer science throughout all PLTW programs. PLTW students are more likely to consider careers as scientists, technology experts, engineers, mathematicians, healthcare providers, and researchers. [1]


1 Pike, Gary and Kirsten Robbins (2014). Using Propensity Scores to Evaluate Education Programs. Indiana University-Purdue University-Indianapolis.


RELEVANCE

Our talented team of educators, higher education experts, policy makers, and industry voices have developed our curriculum so that, within each course, students tackle real-world challenges that include anything from planning a mission to Mars to controlling the COVID-19 pandemic. Their insights have created a learning experience that accurately reflects the needs of the workforce of today and tomorrow, and prepares students for life outside the classroom through:

  • Problem-solving strategies
  • Thinking critically and creatively
  • Communicating and collaborating with others
  • Perseverance
